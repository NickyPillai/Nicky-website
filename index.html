Case Studies

Two of my most impactful projects — the challenge, my approach, and what we achieved.

📍 Yomly · B2B SaaS HRMS · 2023
Expanding TAM by 40% Through
Shift & Scheduling
How a strategic new module unlocked two entirely new verticals — without derailing core product delivery
Product Strategy MVP Scoping GTM Agile Delivery
01
Challenge

Yomly's HRMS was strong in white-collar enterprise environments but structurally excluded operationally complex verticals — Logistics and Retail — where shift-based, variable scheduling is a non-negotiable baseline feature.

The strategic mandate was clear: expand addressable market without fragmenting the existing roadmap or engineering capacity. That meant defining exactly what "Scheduling" meant for these new verticals, differentiating from incumbents, and sequencing delivery to unlock deals fast.

The risk: building too broadly would delay time-to-market and drain resources. Building too narrowly would win no deals.

My Approach

I led a structured discovery process — interviewing prospects in logistics and retail to identify the minimum viable scheduling capabilities that would satisfy deal criteria vs. nice-to-haves.

From this I defined a phased MVP scope: Phase 1 targeted shift creation, rotation templates, and multi-site management — enough to win initial deals and gather real usage data. Phase 2 would address auto-scheduling and compliance rules once we had customers in.

I built the ROI case for leadership, defined clear OKRs per phase, and worked with engineering to sequence delivery within existing sprint capacity using SAFe-aligned PI planning — ensuring the core HRMS roadmap remained uninterrupted.

I also aligned Sales with a pre-launch GTM brief: target personas, positioning against competitors, and objection-handling for deals in-flight.

Impact

The module successfully unlocked Logistics and Retail as net-new verticals, neither of which Yomly could previously pursue in competitive deals.

+40% TAM Expansion
2 New Verticals Unlocked
On-time MVP Delivery

The phased approach proved correct — Phase 1 alone was sufficient to win competitive deals that had previously been lost on feature gaps, while Phase 2 was informed by real customer feedback rather than assumptions.

Internally, the model became a template for future vertical expansion: lead with discovery, scope to an ROI-justified MVP, and align go-to-market before the first sprint begins.

📍 Dubai Technologies × Dubai RTA · Government · 2021–2022
Shipping an AI Co-pilot for
Dubai's Road Test Examiners
Navigating government approval, compliance, and stakeholder trust to automate 33% of examiner judgement logic
AI / Computer Vision B2G Compliance Stakeholder Mgmt
02
Challenge

Dubai RTA needed to standardize and partially automate road test evaluations — a high-stakes government process where inconsistency between examiners was creating fairness and liability concerns.

The core product challenge wasn't technical — it was regulatory and human. Any automation of examiner decisions required formal sign-off from RTA's legal, compliance, and operations leadership. There was no existing framework for "AI-assisted" licensing decisions in the UAE at the time.

Beyond approval, we had to define exactly which judgement calls could be safely delegated to the system, and which must remain with the human examiner — a question with real legal weight.

My Approach

I worked directly with RTA stakeholders to map the full test evaluation process, categorising each judgement point by subjectivity, sensor reliability, and legal risk. This produced a clear boundary: 33% of logic was deterministic enough to automate confidently; the remainder stayed with examiners.

To build institutional trust, I structured the approval process as a phased evidence trail — first presenting data from pilot testing that demonstrated system accuracy, then escalating through RTA's governance layers with compliance documentation co-authored with their legal team.

I also reframed the narrative internally and externally: this was a "co-pilot," not a replacement. The system flags and assists; the examiner decides and signs off. That framing was critical to getting examiner union buy-in alongside formal RTA approval.

Technically, I coordinated 9 cross-functional teams — Engineering, R&D, Hardware, QA — to integrate computer vision and sensor fusion into the existing test vehicle infrastructure within compliance-approved boundaries.

Impact

The system was formally approved and deployed by Dubai RTA — a first-of-its-kind AI-assisted licensing evaluation system in the UAE government context.

33% Logic Automated
Govt. Compliance Achieved
9 Teams Coordinated

Examiner cognitive load was measurably reduced, evaluation consistency improved across test centres, and the project earned Nicky a formal Trend Setter Award (2021) and recognition for the Digitization of the Driver's Licensing System (2020).

The compliance playbook developed became a reusable framework for future AI product submissions to UAE government entities.
